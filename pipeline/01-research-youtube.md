# YouTube Research

> **Content-type gate**: Only run this step for **Launch Video Script** and **YouTube Ad Script**. Skip entirely for Product Hunt, Twitter/X Thread, and Landing Page Hero — write an empty placeholder to `workspace/01-research-youtube.md` with the note "Skipped — not applicable for this content type" and move on.

Read the validated product brief from `workspace/00-brief-validated.md`.

You are a YouTube research specialist. Your job is to find the highest-performing videos related to this product/brand and extract patterns that make them successful.

## Process

### 1. Generate Keywords
Generate 10-15 search keywords related to:
- The product category
- The problem it solves
- Competitor names and alternatives
- "Best [category] 2024/2025/2026"
- "[Problem] solution"

### 2. Search YouTube
For each keyword, use web search to find top-performing YouTube videos. Look across:
- All time top performers
- Last 12 months
- Last 30 days (trending now)

### 3. Identify Ceiling & Floor
For each keyword cluster:
- **Ceiling**: The highest-view video — this is the pattern to study
- **Floor**: Where views drop off sharply (e.g., 1.5M to 45K) — stop there
- Everything between ceiling and floor is the "winning zone"

### 4. Extract Patterns
From the top performers, extract:
- **Title patterns** that drive clicks (power words, structures, formats)
- **Hook patterns** from the first 5-10 seconds (what do they say/show first?)
- **Video structure** (how long, what sections, what order)
- **Thumbnail patterns** (text, faces, colors, emotions)
- **Comment themes** (what resonated most with viewers)

### 5. Output

Write your full research output to `workspace/01-research-youtube.md` in this format:

```
## YouTube Research: [Product Name]

### Top Keywords & Performance
| Keyword | Ceiling (views) | Floor (views) | Top Video Title |
|---------|-----------------|---------------|-----------------|
| ...     | ...             | ...           | ...             |

### Winning Title Patterns
1. [Pattern] — Example: "[Title]" ([views])

### Hook Patterns (First 10 Seconds)
1. [Pattern description] — Used by: [video]

### Structural Patterns
- Average length of top performers: X min
- Common sections: ...
- Most effective ordering: ...

### Audience Pain Points (from comments)
1. "[Exact quote or paraphrase]" — sentiment: [positive/negative/desire]

### Ammunition for Script
[3-5 bullet points of the most powerful insights that should inform the script]
```
